Q&A


Q1:
Three essential differences between Web1, Web2, and Web3 are:
1. Site Contributers: While the only Web1 users who could edit or change the site are the owners of Web2 sites, the interface of
the sites changed and allowed visitors to comment and edit certain sites (eg Wikipedia) and Web3 provides changes to sites using machines and algorithms that change the face of the site according to users Who visit this site
2. Development technologies: While Web1 development technologies had languages ​​such as: php,
html, css, ruby ​​(especially on the client side) in Web2 the world has changed to use scripts (javaScript)
and the use of other flash that make a significant difference between non-dynamic programming and dynamic programming.
Web3 added additional technologies such as artificial intelligence, which is expressed in the construction of the site in a
manner suitable for users different according to the algorithm used by site owners.
3. Concepts that distinguish the web: While web1 most of the sites were built in the form of "home page",
in web2 the most common sites that were established were in the form of "blog" or "wikis" and
web3 most common pages are crafted "live streem" or "weves".


Q2
Explanation of the differences by the following use case: Search for vacation online:
In Web1: Search for a company that sells vacations abroad, finding a website and contacting an agent via
phone / email and closing your vacation.
In Web2 Search for a company that sells holidays to the sand, finding a site from a variety of sites and searching for a
suitable vacation according to dates and other constraints, buying on the site itself.
In Web3: Search for a company that sells holidays to the sand, finding a site with a variety of vacation sites with an
artificial intelligence algorithm that checks which vacation will be the best and most beneficial to the user to purchase
according to the constraints entered.
